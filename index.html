About Us

The RAVN Project was founded by a group of Hopkinton High School students in an Aerospace Engineering club in 2011. After graduating from high school, the leaders of the group aka us, decided to take the project further and finalize the design. The project is now funded by multiple sponsors and we have been given the privilege of working on the plane in the MIT Aerospace Labs. We will continue to work on the plane around our studies and we plan to have a working prototype in the very near future. Thanks you so much for your interest!

Propulsion

Three 120 degree pivoting duct fan units are placed in an equilaterally triangular configuration. These engines are 68 mm , 4000kv, 4S fan, 60,000 RPM duct fans.

Testing Frame

This is an early build of the frame we use for vertical hovering tests. The three duct fans pivot in a 120 degree range and are spread to three locations on the frame that are equidistant from the center of gravity. For now we use this test frame to finish our programming and calculations for stable vertical flight. An outer shell will eventually be made to generate lift when in forward flight. (See below)

The New Frame

To make the plane lighter and stronger, we have designed a new frame in CAD that is ready to be printed via a 3D mill. The frame will be made from a carbon fiber coated depron foam and will allow us to mount the motors, servos, batteries, and other electronics with less extra parts. In addition, the outer shell will fit nicely around the frame.

Outer Shell

These are the latest CAD designs of the outer shell. The wide fiberglass fuselage forms a lifting body as well as providing space for payload. We designed a blended wing body to minimize drag and reach high air speeds.

Flight Control System

The ArduPilot Mega is the heart of the flight control system. It runs on open source software that provides UAV functionality including waypoints, ground station control and more. We communicate wirelessly to the ArduPilot using an Xbee Telemetry Kit. The ArduPilot provides us the microcontroller functionality that we need to control all of the servos and motors on board the plane. Additionally the ArduPilot provides the plane with sensors so that it knows it's heading and location. We have scraped the opensource pilot code and written our own to stabilize our custom built plane in air and provide a transition from vertical to horizontal flight mode.
ArduPilot Mega Features:
3-axis Accelerometer
3-axis Gyro
Barometer
16 MHz ATMega2560 processor
ATMega328 processor
GPS

Rotor Craft

Rotorcraft such as helicopters and quadrocopters are great for taking off and landing vertically as well as their agility, but they are limited in their ability to accelerate on the horizontal plane.

Fixed Wing Aircraft

Planes are able to achieve very high horizontal speeds and are able to travel long distances, but they require long takeoff/landing strips in order to get in or out of the air.

The RAVN

The RAVN provides us with a design that is the best of both worlds with the limitations of neither. Being both agile and fast in all directional dimensions makes the RAVN superior.
